Screen Space Directional Occlusion

This is what I worked on during my third year internship at Hapticmedia. The Screen Space Directional Occlusion (SSDO) is a type of Screen Space Ambiant Occlusion (SSAO).

Approximating Dynamic Global Illumination in Image Space, T. Ritschel, T. Grosch, H-P. Seidel

SSAO

Classic SSAO simulates contact shadows in image space by computing the amount of incoming light on a certain point P on a surface. It does so by using a small group of points arange in an hemisphere oriented around P. Using the poisition of those points, the ratio of the number points under the surrounding surface against the number of points above it is calculated, which the can be used to dim the default darken the surface to simulate contact shadows.

Left: Albedo. Center: SSAO pass. Right: Albedo + SSAO

SSDO

The SSDO algorithme uses the same basis, but it uses the color framebuffer to compute the influence of neaby colored surfaces, producing colored contact shadows. SSDO is also capable, by using the normal framebuffer of the influence points, to simulated indirect lighting at short range. During my internship, I implemented the SSDO, the I extended it to take into account the environement lighting.

Automated Mesh Segmentation

Another University project, where the goal was, given a sample of scanned trees and plants, to divide the plant in three types of sub-meshes : trunk, branches and leaves. We choose a skeletonization algorithm, which decimate the mesh to create a skeleton representing the overall shape. It also stores, for each new vertex created to construct the skeleton, the list of the decimated vertices that helped create it. With those data, we planned to run further analysis to determine to which categories those vertices belong to. However, due to short deadlines and faulty geometric data-structure, we only got partial results (albeit with very short computation times)

Decomposing Polygon Meshes for Interactive Applications, Xuetao Li, Tong Wing Woon, Tiow Seng Tan and Zhiyong Huang
